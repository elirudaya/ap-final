pure, undistracted experience. if other capsules are tools, this space is closer to a state of deep sleep or meditation. complete isolation from the outside world, no visual noise, not even tactile sensations—just the user’s consciousness and a selected memory, amplified to incredible intensity.
here, you can relive a first kiss, feeling every moment as vividly as if it were happening for the first time. or practice a complex violin piece until your fingers memorize every movement at a muscle-memory level. Some users report that time flows differently inside—minutes stretch into hours.
but this depth comes at a cost: a 48-hour recovery period is required after each session. The Local Immersion Capsule is the most powerful and demanding tool in the system, and it must be handled with care.

This is a dynamic space where memories exist in their most fragile and volatile state. The capsule operates with high-speed neural buffers, allowing users to relive recent events, fleeting impressions, and even thoughts that never fully formed into clear images.
The atmosphere inside is one of constant motion: holograms of memories flicker, overlap, and sometimes distort like reflections in rippling water. This space is ideal for psychologists studying the mechanisms of forgetting or investigators reconstructing events from fragmented eyewitness accounts.
The system actively cools itself—a soft hum of ventilation serves as a reminder that the brain, like any machine, needs breaks. Sessions here are shorter than in other capsules but more intense: short-term memory resists prolonged interference.

A vault of everything we call "the self." Unlike the fleeting impressions of short-term memory, here, memories take shape, accumulating details and emotions. But they are not static—over time, some fragments fade into digital "noise," while others grow even more vivid.
The capsule’s interior resembles an infinite archive, where key life moments are highlighted like important documents, while peripheral ones fade into the background. The walls react to the emotional tone of memories: warm golden patterns for joyful moments, blue ripples for calm ones, sharp red flashes for painful ones.
Working with long-term memory requires caution—excessive interference can "contaminate" the original memories. Before each session, the system performs meticulous calibration, and the process itself feels more like meditative immersion than rapid switching between events.

A laboratory for thought. This space is designed not so much for revisiting the past as for engaging with the present—users can simulate scenarios, solve complex problems, or even form "cognitive ensembles" for collective thinking.
Inside, an atmosphere of creative chaos reigns: thought holograms take the form of 3D diagrams, rapidly shifting schematics, or abstract shapes pulsing in sync with neural activity. The system highlights zones of heightened focus—areas where users direct their attention.
But there are limits: prolonged use leads to mental exhaustion, so the capsule is equipped with biofeedback mechanisms. When the brain is overworked, users feel gentle pulses—a reminder that even the most advanced interface requires rest.
